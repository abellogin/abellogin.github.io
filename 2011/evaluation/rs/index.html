Evaluation Project for Recommender Systems

There is considerable methodological divergence in the way precision-oriented metrics are being applied in the Recommender Systems field, and as a consequence, the results reported in different studies are difficult to put in context and compare. We have compared five experimental methodologies, broadly covering the variants reported in the literature. In our experiments with three state-of-the-art recommenders, four of the evaluation methodologies are consistent with each other and differ from error metrics, in terms of the comparative recommenders' performance measurements. The other procedure aligns with RMSE, but shows a heavy bias towards known relevant items, considerably overestimating performance.

Here, we present the framework used to obtain the above results, along with some documentation and additional examples. Our framework has been built on top of the Apache Mahout library, although other libraries can be easily evaluated since we include a file-based wrapper for the Recommender's interface. An example with the output files generated by the MyMediaLite library is presented below.

Finally, the generated files could be processed by the trec_eval program, in order to obtain the standard IR evaluation metrics, such as precision, recall, or nDCG. Because of this, the standard output for the recommender files is as follows: 


Query_Id \t 'Q0' \t Doc_Id \t Ranking_Pos \t Score \t Run_Name

For recommendation purposes, queries are interpreted as users, and documents as items. Thus, the following line 


543	Q0	57	1	5.0	UB

represents that the UB recommender has predicted a score of 5.0 towards item 57 for the user 543. Besides, this item would have the first position in the corresponding ranking.
